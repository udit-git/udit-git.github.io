What's different about us: Our Process
  • Step 1: Detailed Examination of Needs

    Advanced tools and analytics are used to analyze and look for existing solutions that may help.
    Eg: A failing toothpaste business. We analyze its social media presence and books of accounts and see customer traffic and reviews are optimal.

  • Step 2: Black Board Testing

    The existing solutions are compared with each other, and the one that fits client needs best is chosen.
    Eg: The proposed solutions- Customer Interview, Self-Testing, Outsourced market research are compared. We choose to do Self-Testing first.

  • Step 3: Drafts Created

    After the proposed solutions are tried out, their results are documented. These are scrutinized for further analysis.
    Eg: All testers after buying the ice cream point out 4 common points: It is comfortable to use, affordable, courteously served, and well packaged.

  • Step 4: Drafts Scrutinized

    After analyzing the above 4 points for positives and negatives. This is necessary as any factors can have effects in any way, good or bad.
    Eg. Of testing the 4 points, it is seen that there is no obvious flaw. And thus it is proposed to review product turnover (how much product customer uses, and in how much time).

  • Step 5: Corrective Measures devised

    The points from the above are worked upon again and again to find the best foot forward. This reduces the possibility of mistake and increases probability of success.
    Eg: It is seen that very few bars of toothpaste are used in comparison to others, even by regular customers. On inspection, we see that the tube opening is tiny, thus the tube lasts relatively longer.

  • Step 6: Final Review

    The proposed solution, before being given to the client, is shown to experts for their opinion and review. This ensures client success and efficiency via the solution. Eg: A group of international market research analysts are called to discuss the proposed solution. After their go-ahead, we tell client to increase size of tube opening, thus increasing product turnover.
